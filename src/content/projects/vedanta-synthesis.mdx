An ongoing project to specify, rather than assert, where Vedantic metaphysics converges structurally with modern physics and cognitive science. The methodological commitment is that analogies aren't arguments: Brahman is not equivalent to "consciousness" as a notion, and vacuum energy is not identical to akasha — unless you can name the mechanism that makes the mapping non-trivial.

## Sub-papers on the table

1. **"Vedantic Manas and Attention: A Cognitive Phenomenology"** — the most executable of the set. Meditators as subjects, attention metrics (SART / ANT) against baseline, phenomenological report as structured data.
2. **"Gauge Invariance and the Vedantic Distinction Between Apparent and Real"** — ~2k words, structurally complete. A weekend to draft.
3. **"Vivekananda, Schrödinger, and the Measurement Problem"** — ~8k words. Target: religious studies or philosophy-of-physics venue.
4. **Textbook chapter on three-layer reality (Paramarthika / Vyavaharika / Pratibhasika)** — educational, not novel research.

## What the project is not

Not numerology (archetypal ratios onto cosmological data — tried, dissolved). Not unfalsifiable identity claims (zero-point energy = akasha — tempting, empty without a mechanism). Not market-timing-via-Jungian-archetypes (200+ conversations without a single backtest).

## Status

Private notes and drafts in the project vault. The flagship piece — a treatment of Jung's *vāk* mantra and neural entrainment — is the first thing I expect to ship publicly.
